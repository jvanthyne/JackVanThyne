2004- NOW I’m 21, a student of landscape architecture, and I learn by making. My work often begins with wood, drawn to its tactile qualities and the quiet stories it carries, though metal, light, film, and music also shape the way I explore space, objects, and experience. I am interested in the small shifts that change how we see and feel — moments when familiar things reveal something new. Each project carries a sense of time, both in how it is built and in how it might evolve, be reused, or continue to exist in new ways. Sustainability is never separate from this work. It is a way of paying attention, of noticing the relationships between materials, people, and place, and letting that awareness guide each choice. My practice is about curiosity, care, and creating spaces and objects that invite reflection, connection, and engagement with the world around us.
