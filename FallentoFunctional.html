Fallen to Functional

Community Spaces from Campus Wood Waste.

Overview

Fallen to Functional: Community Spaces from Campus Wood Waste is a design-build project at UC Berkeley that reclaims wood from trees removed for safety, age, or disease. The project fabricates four outdoor furniture pieces for underused campus areas, using material that would otherwise be discarded.

Each piece is produced at the Richmond Field Station Wood Lab and installed with on-site signage that documents the tree’s species, approximate age, origin, and reason for removal. The project tracks wood volume diverted, number and distribution of pieces, estimated embodied carbon savings, and feedback from students and staff who use the new spaces.

Goals

  • Sustainability: divert usable wood from disposal and reduce embodied carbon.
  • Connection to place: construct furniture from the campus landscape itself.
  • Community: provide informal, accessible outdoor spaces for everyday use.
  • Education: use plaques and QR codes to describe circular design and material reuse.
  • Scalability: test a framework for a potential Campus Wood Reuse Program.

Timeline (Dec 2025 – May 2026)

Dec – Jan

Identify removed trees, select usable logs, and mill lumber at the Wood Lab for four furniture pieces.

Target: complete milling and rough dimensioning for all four pieces by late January.

Feb – Mar

Develop final designs and fabricate the furniture. Test joinery, finishes, and hardware suitable for outdoor conditions.

Target: complete fabrication of at least two pieces by mid-March; all four pieces by the end of March.

Apr

Install furniture in approved campus locations with plaques and QR-code signage.

Target: two installations completed by early April; remaining two installed by the end of April.

May

Document each installation, photograph the work, and compile material for this archive and final reporting.

Target: complete documentation and post-installation observations by late May.
