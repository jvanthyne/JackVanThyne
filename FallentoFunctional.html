Overview

Fallen to Functional: Community Spaces from Campus Wood Waste is a design-build project at UC Berkeley. The project reclaims wood from trees removed for safety, age, or disease and fabricates benches, tables, and seating clusters for underused outdoor areas.

All pieces are produced at the Richmond Field Station Wood Lab and installed as durable, place-based infrastructure that links campus furniture to campus ecology.

Goals

  • Sustainability: divert usable wood from disposal and reduce embodied carbon.
  • Connection to place: construct furniture from the campus landscape itself.
  • Community: provide inviting, low-barrier outdoor spaces for everyday use.
  • Education: use plaques and QR codes to communicate circular design and material reuse.
  • Scalability: pilot a framework for a future Campus Wood Reuse Program.

Memorial Glade Bench Cluster

The Memorial Glade Bench Cluster uses reclaimed wood from a removed campus tree to create a set of low benches oriented toward the central lawn. The installation provides informal seating for study and events while keeping the material story visible.

Eucalyptus Grove Table

The Eucalyptus Grove Table is fabricated from salvaged eucalyptus, forming a shared work surface along the edge of Strawberry Creek. The piece supports group work and informal gatherings while referencing the history of the surrounding grove.

North Gate Entry Seating

The North Gate Entry Seating frames the approach to the College of Environmental Design with compact elements built from reclaimed campus wood. The installation offers a defined pause point at a busy threshold between city and campus.
