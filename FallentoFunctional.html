Fallen to Functional

Community Spaces from Campus Wood Waste — a student-led circular design initiative.
Overview• Goals• Timeline• Team• Impact• Archive

Project Overview

Fallen to Functional: Community Spaces from Campus Wood Waste is a design-build initiative that reimagines how UC Berkeley manages its natural resources. The project transforms fallen and discarded campus trees into functional outdoor furniture and community gathering spaces.

Using the Richmond Field Station Wood Lab, the project reclaims wood from removed trees and fabricates benches, tables, and seating clusters for underutilized outdoor areas.

Goals & Objectives

  • Sustainability: Divert usable wood from disposal and reduce embodied carbon.
  • Connection to Place: Celebrate UC Berkeley’s natural identity through reclaimed materials.
  • Community: Create accessible gathering spaces for students and staff.
  • Education: Use QR-linked signage to teach circular design and material reuse.
  • Scalability: Establish a framework for future campus wood-reuse projects.

Implementation Timeline (Dec 2025 → May 2026)

Dec – Jan

Select trees, transport logs, mill lumber, and prepare material.

Feb – Mar

Design and fabricate benches, tables, and seating clusters.

Apr

Install furniture in underused campus areas with plaques and QR codes.

May

Complete documentation, photography, and public website archive.
