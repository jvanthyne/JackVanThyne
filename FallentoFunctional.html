Community Spaces from Campus Wood Waste. A student-led project that transforms fallen campus trees into outdoor furniture and gathering spaces.

Project Overview

Fallen to Functional: Community Spaces from Campus Wood Waste reclaims wood from trees removed on UC Berkeley’s campus and turns it into benches, tables, and seating clusters for underused outdoor areas.

Each piece is fabricated at the Richmond Field Station Wood Lab and installed as durable, place-based infrastructure that reconnects students with the landscape that surrounds them.

Goals

  • Sustainability: divert usable wood from disposal and reduce embodied carbon.
  • Connection to place: build furniture from the campus landscape itself.
  • Community: provide inviting outdoor spaces for gathering and reflection.
  • Education: share stories of circular design and material reuse.
  • Scalability: pilot a framework for a Campus Wood Reuse Program.

Memorial Glade Bench Cluster

The Memorial Glade Bench Cluster uses reclaimed wood from a diseased campus tree to create a series of low benches oriented toward the central lawn. The design provides informal seating for study, conversation, and events while keeping the material story visible.

Eucalyptus Grove Table

The Eucalyptus Grove Table is fabricated from salvaged eucalyptus, creating a long shared surface adjacent to Strawberry Creek. The piece supports group work and informal gatherings while referencing the history of the surrounding grove.

North Gate Entry Seating

The North Gate Entry Seating frames the approach to the College of Environmental Design with compact seating elements built from reclaimed campus wood. The installation offers a clear pause point at a busy threshold between city and campus.
