ABOUT ME — 2004–NOW INTRODUCTION I’M JACK VAN THYNE, A 21-YEAR-OLD DESIGN STUDENT, ARTIST, AND BUILDER. I STUDY LANDSCAPE ARCHITECTURE AND SUSTAINABLE DESIGN AT UC BERKELEY, WITH A FOCUS ON FURNITURE DESIGN AND HANDS-ON BUILDING. MEDIUM AND MATERIALS WOOD IS MY FAVORITE MEDIUM BECAUSE OF ITS NATURAL, TACTILE QUALITIES. IT CARRIES A DEEP HUMAN CONNECTION — YOU CAN SEE WHERE IT COMES FROM, FEEL ITS GRAIN, AND SENSE ITS HISTORY. I ALSO WORK WITH METAL, LIGHT, WATERCOLOR, FILM, AND MUSIC, EACH OFFERING A UNIQUE ROLE IN MY CREATIVE PROCESS. ARTISTIC PHILOSOPHY THE GOAL OF MY WORK IS TO CURATE FEELING THROUGH DESIGN — CREATING ART, SPACES, AND OBJECTS THAT SHIFT PERSPECTIVES AND RESHAPE HOW WE EXPERIENCE THE WORLD. I AIM TO BRING FRESH VIEWS TO THE FAMILIAR, SPARKING MOMENTS OF EMOTIONAL RESONANCE AND DEEPER ENGAGEMENT. I STRIVE TO DESIGN PROJECTS THAT ARE BUILT TO LAST WHILE ALSO CONSIDERING THEIR AFTERLIFE — HOW A PIECE CAN EVOLVE, BE REUSED, OR FIND NEW PURPOSE. SUSTAINABILITY SUSTAINABILITY IS CENTRAL TO MY DESIGN PROCESS. FOR ME, IT’S ABOUT BUILDING A THOUGHTFUL RELATIONSHIP WITH THE WORLD AROUND US — ENSURING THAT SUSTAINABLE PRACTICES GUIDE EACH DECISION, FROM MATERIAL CHOICES TO CONSTRUCTION METHODS, SO THAT EVERY PROJECT RESPECTS BOTH PEOPLE AND PLACE.
